<p><p>Computed Tomography (CT) is a medical imaging technique that uses X-rays to create detailed cross-sectional images of the body. It combines multiple X-ray images taken from different angles and processes them using computer algorithms to produce comprehensive views of internal organs and structures, aiding in accurate diagnosis and treatment planning.</p><p>The CT market is witnessing significant growth, driven by advancements in imaging technology, increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, and a rising geriatric population. Hospitals and diagnostic centers are increasingly adopting CT scanners due to their ability to provide rapid and precise imaging, enhancing patient care. The demand for portable and advanced CT devices is also on the rise, catering to the need for improved accessibility in remote areas.</p><p>Additionally, the integration of artificial intelligence in CT imaging is enhancing diagnostic accuracy and workflow efficiency. Other trends include the growing emphasis on preventive care and the expansion of applications in oncology, cardiology, and neurology, which is broadening the scope of CT imaging.
